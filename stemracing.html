STEM Racing

Where engineering meets motorsport. Students design, build, and race CO₂-powered cars — competing from regional qualifiers all the way to the World Finals.

What is
STEM Racing?

Formerly known as F1 in Schools, STEM Racing is a global competition where teams of students aged 9–19 design, manufacture, and race miniature CO₂-powered cars. It blends real-world engineering with the prestige of Formula 1 motorsport.

Teams work through every stage of a real engineering project: from CAD design and CNC manufacturing to branding, sponsorship, and live technical presentations — just like a professional race team.

Endorsed by Formula 1 and run in over 30 countries, it's one of the most complete STEM challenges available to high school students anywhere in the world.

Engineering

Teams analyse drag, thrust, and mass to push their car's performance to the theoretical limit.

🎨

Branding

From team identity and livery to sponsorship portfolios, marketing is a scored part of competition.

🗣️

Presentation

Teams present to a panel of industry judges, defending every design decision made.

🏁

Racing

Cars race head-to-head down a 20-metre track powered by a CO₂ cartridge, hitting speeds up to 100 km/h.
