What is
STEM Racing?

Formerly known as F1 in Schools, STEM Racing is a global competition where teams of students aged 9–19 design, manufacture, and race miniature CO₂-powered cars. It blends real-world engineering with the prestige of Formula 1 motorsport.

Teams work through every stage of a real engineering project: from CAD design and CNC manufacturing to branding, sponsorship, and live technical presentations — just like a professional race team.

Endorsed by Formula 1 and run in over 30 countries, it's one of the most complete STEM challenges available to high school students anywhere in the world.

Visit STEM Racing ↗
01
Engineering

Design & Engineering

Teams use industry-standard CAD software to design aerodynamic car bodies, running CFD simulations to optimise every surface for drag and downforce. Every gram and every millimetre matters — cars are engineered to hit speeds up to 100 km/h over a 20-metre track powered by a CO₂ cartridge.

02
Manufacturing

Precision Manufacturing

From digital model to physical car, teams use CNC machines to carve their designs from balsa wood blocks to fractions of a millimetre. The manufacturing process demands precision, patience, and a deep understanding of materials — skills straight from real-world production floors.

03
Marketing

Branding & Marketing

STEM Racing isn't just about speed — teams build a full brand identity, secure real sponsorships, and present a professional portfolio to industry judges. From livery design to verbal presentations and pit displays, marketing is a scored and critical part of every competition.
